The history of Orrisa begins in the Lower Paleolithic era, as Acheulian tools dating to the period have been discovered in various places in the region. The early history of Orrisa can be traced back to writings found in ancient texts like the Mahabharata, Maha Govinda Sutta, and some Puranas.

* Jagannath Temple: The Jagannath Temple is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Jagannath, a form of the god Vishnu. It is located in Puri, Orrisa, on the eastern coast of India. The temple is an important pilgrimage destination and one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites for Hindus.

Additional Places in Orrisa

* Puri: Famous for the Jagannath Temple, one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites for Hindus. The annual Rath Yatra (chariot festival) in Puri attracts millions of devotees.

* Chilika Lake: Asia's largest brackish water lagoon, famous for its scenic beauty and diverse birdlife. It's a significant wintering ground for migratory birds.

* Raghurajpur: A heritage crafts village known for its traditional Pattachitra paintings, palm leaf engravings, and Odissi dance and music.
